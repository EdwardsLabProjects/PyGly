== Introduction ==

Glycan nonreducing-end alignment requires motif structures align with at least one connected substructure (subtree) at the nonreducing end of the glycan. Aligned motif and glycan monosaccharides and glycosidic linkages must respect the matching rules outlined below. Additional phosphate and sulfate substituents on glycan monosaccharides are permitted, even when the motif does not indicate them. When ambiguity in glycan topology permits at least one motif-to-substructure alignment, the motif and glycan are considered to align.

Motif alignment to the non-reducing end of the glycan is achieved by requiring the motif non-reducing end monosaccharides align to glycan non-reducing end monosaccharides. 

Currently, nonreducing-end alignments are only used in the GlyGen motif collection.
