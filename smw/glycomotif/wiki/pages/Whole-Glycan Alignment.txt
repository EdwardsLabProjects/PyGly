== Introduction ==

Whole-glycan alignment requires motif structures align with the entire glycan. Aligned motif and glycan monosaccharides and glycosidic linkages must respect the matching rules outlined below. Additional phosphate and sulfate substituents on glycan monosaccharides and alditol modifications at the reducing end of the glycan are NOT permitted, unlike the other alignment strategies. When ambiguity in glycan topology permits at least one motif-to-substructure alignment, the motif and glycan are considered to align. 

Whole-glycan alignment is primarily used to assign semantic names to specific glycan structures. 

Currently, whole-glycan alignments are only used in the GlyGen motif collection.
